About Me

Hello, my name is John Faulkner and I'm currently a senior in my game design program. I've loved video games as a kid. My first console was the NES, but it wasn't until I got a PlayStation 1 that I played games on a regular basis. I enjoyed games so much that it became more than a hobby, it was my passion. The one game that influence me to go into Game Design was Metroid Prime. It was a game that perfectly balanced art, story, and design to create one of the best experiences I ever had. One day I hope to make games as good as Metroid Prime.

What do I do?

When it comes to video games, I am a artist and storyteller. I am pretty efficient in 2D art, and have some experience with making 3D art in Maya. I also enjoy writing storys for games and developing the characters beyond a steroetypical scope. One day I hope to write a compelling narative to a even more compelling and fun video game.

Creating pages manually

If you prefer to not use the automatic generator, push a branch named gh-pages to your repository to create a page manually. In addition to supporting regular HTML content, GitHub Pages support Jekyll, a simple, blog aware static site generator. Jekyll makes it easy to create site-wide headers and footers without having to copy them across every page. It also offers intelligent blog support and other advanced templating features.
